10 Resume Optimization Tips That Will Get You More Interviews in 2026

7 min read  ·  ResumeChangers Team

Most resumes fail for the same handful of reasons. Weak bullet points. Missing keywords. Generic summaries. Poor formatting. The good news: these are all fixable. Here are 10 changes you can make today that will directly improve your interview rate.

Tip 01

Tailor your resume for every application

A generic resume performs poorly across the board. The highest-converting resumes are customized to each specific job posting — matching the language, keywords, and priorities of that role. Yes, it takes more time. Yes, it's worth it. Tailored resumes get significantly more callbacks than generic ones.

Tip 02

Quantify every achievement you possibly can

Numbers grab attention and add credibility. Replace vague claims with measurable results wherever possible.

Before
"Managed social media accounts and grew the following"
After
"Grew Instagram following from 4K to 22K in 8 months, increasing profile link clicks by 340%"
Tip 03

Lead every bullet point with a strong action verb

Start each bullet with a powerful, specific verb. Avoid weak openers like "Responsible for," "Helped with," or "Worked on." Instead use: Spearheaded, Engineered, Reduced, Generated, Negotiated, Launched, Optimized, Delivered.

Before
"Responsible for managing the onboarding process"
After
"Redesigned onboarding process, cutting time-to-productivity for new hires by 30%"
Tip 04

Use a clean, single-column format

Multi-column resumes look modern but break ATS parsers. The system reads left to right, top to bottom — columns cause your content to be read in the wrong order or skipped entirely. Stick to a single column with clear section headings.

Tip 05

Add a targeted summary at the top

A 2-3 sentence professional summary at the top of your resume sets the context for everything below it. Make it specific to the role you're applying for — not a generic "results-oriented professional." Include your job title, years of experience, and your biggest relevant strength.

Before
"Motivated professional seeking new opportunities to leverage my skills"
After
"Digital Marketing Manager with 5 years driving paid acquisition for B2B SaaS companies. Reduced CAC by 40% at my last role while scaling monthly pipeline from $200K to $1.2M."
Tip 06

Match keywords exactly from the job description

ATS systems look for exact or near-exact matches. "Microsoft Excel" and "Excel" may be treated differently. "Project Management" and "managing projects" may not match. Use the exact phrasing from the job posting whenever possible.

Tip 07

Keep it to one or two pages maximum

Unless you have 15+ years of experience, aim for one page. Two pages is acceptable for senior roles. Anything longer gets skimmed, not read. Cut old or irrelevant experience ruthlessly — your resume from 2009 is not helping you get a job in 2026.

Tip 08

Include a Skills section with specific tools and technologies

A dedicated Skills section near the top is one of the first things ATS systems and recruiters scan. List hard skills, software, platforms, and certifications. Don't list soft skills like "good communicator" — these are expected and waste valuable space.

Tip 09

Use standard section headings

ATS parsers are trained to recognize standard section names. Use: "Experience" or "Work Experience," "Education," "Skills," "Certifications." Avoid creative headings like "My Journey" or "Where I've Worked" — the ATS may not categorize these correctly.

Tip 10

Check your ATS score before submitting

Before you hit send on any application, check how your resume scores against that specific job description. A score below 60 means you're likely to be filtered out. Aim for 75 or above. This single habit will dramatically improve your interview rate.

The compound effect: Each of these tips has a modest impact on its own. Applied together to a resume tailored for a specific role, they can take your ATS score from the 20s to the 80s — and dramatically increase the chance a real human reads your application.
